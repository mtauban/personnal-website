Hello !

My name is Mathieu Tauban (*) and I'm a PhD student in Physics. I'm interested in understanding the origin of non-linear mechanical behaviour of nanofilled elastomers. My mission is to extend the Glassy Bridge Reinforcement Model in order to account for the complex morphology of real fillers.

My day-to-day life

My research field concerns the mechanical behaviour of nanocomposites. I extensively use numerical simulation in order to build a model that allows us to understand the behaviour of filled elastomers and thermoplastic nanocomposites when the filler has a nanometric dimension. The comprehension of reinforcing mechanism are key to taylor the mechanical properties of the material to fil specific needs and industrial application.

My work rely on numerical simulations. I developped a C++ program for the mesoscale simulation of our materials. I also use a lot of tools that are commercial and open-source. In order to illustrate my point, let's mention Mathematica, Matlab, Awk, Gnuplot, VLC, etc... I usually work on stable environment (ie. Linux) on high performance scientific clusters. For example, I work on two clusters with different job scheduler. I worked around the limitation of PBS and SLURM and came up with a general script that adapt itself to the environment. This ended my headache when transposing simulation from a cluster to the other one.

I like to use the tool that suits my needs and this is why I'm always happy to try new numerical simulation software, visualisation tools, ...
